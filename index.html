Upper Hill Nairobi

Nairobi is an energetic modern city that serves as a fascinating introduction to both wildlife and travel, Nairobi is the capital city of Kenya. Nairobi is home to beautiful national park and is filled with the most amazing people and culture. A fun fact is that Nairobi National Park is the only national park that borders a capital city. Nairobi is nicknamed both “the green city in the sun” and “the safari capital of the world.” One thing to keep in mind when traveling to Nairobi is that it is generally safe during the day but during the night, it is a high-risk area. It’s best to stay vigilant and practice common sense and safety at night. Nairobi’s name is derived from the Maasai phrase “Enkare Nairobi,” which translates to “cool water. Upper Hill is a neighborhood of the city of Nairobi, the capital and largest city of Kenya. Upper Hill is located in the financial district of Upper Hill and surrounded by banks, international corporate companies, Embassies and in close proximity of the Jomo Kenyatta International Airport and Uhuru Park, Upper Hill district has seen an increase in major construction in recent years, with many multinational corporations setting up offices. Upper Hill is located 2 kilometers by road west of the Nairobi central business district. The coordinates of Upper Hill are: 1°17'59.0"S, 36°48'58.0"E (Latitude: -1.299719; Longitude:36.816097). Upper Hill lies in two major Nairobi City sub-counties; Westlands and Ngong Road separates the two sub-counties. Upper Hill once small district that boasted to be a residential area for senior civil servants especially has transformed over the years to become one of the most sought-after areas to set up a thriving business. Welcome to Upper Hill in Nairobi the home of lucrative businesses with changing skylines thanks to the exquisite skyscrapers that have popped up over the years. Nairobi Upper Hill was historically one of the residential neighborhoods of the Nairobi city during colonial times, and stretched all the way to the Kilimani estate, the 280 hectares that comprise Upper Hill were majorly owned by Kenya Railways. During the 1990s and early 2000s, as land and office space became scarce and exorbitantly priced in the central business district, businesses relocated to Upper Hill and Westlands, where land and office space were more readily available and less expensive. Today, Upper Hill possesses a new modern skyline that is still being filled by modern skyscrapers of different and prestigious designs in the architectural world.
